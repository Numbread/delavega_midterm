Places you must visit in Surigao City

Siargao Island

Siargao Island, nestled in the Philippines, epitomizes a tropical paradise renowned for its multifaceted allure. Dubbed the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," it magnetizes surf enthusiasts worldwide with its iconic Cloud 9, an acclaimed surf break yielding exhilarating waves. Yet, beyond its surfing prowess, Siargao captivates with its breathtaking natural splendor. Pristine beaches, adorned with powdery sands and azure waters, beckon visitors to explore its coastal treasures.

Mubua Pebble Beach

Mubua Pebble Beach, located in Siargao Island, Philippines, is known for its unique and stunning natural beauty. Unlike traditional sandy beaches, Mubua features smooth, rounded pebbles that stretch along its shoreline, creating a picturesque and distinctive seascape. The beach provides a unique coastal experience for visitors to Siargao Island, offering a serene and scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Whether it's to admire the natural beauty, relax in the tranquil surroundings, or simply enjoy a quiet moment by the sea, Mubua Pebble Beach promises an unforgettable coastal retreat.

Hagonoy Island Beach

Hagonoy Island Beach, nestled in Siargao Island, Philippines, is celebrated for its untouched natural beauty and serene ambiance. Blessed with powdery white sands, crystal-clear waters, and swaying coconut palm trees, Hagonoy Island Beach embodies the epitome of a tropical paradise. Visitors can also experience the warmth and hospitality of the local community, as small vendors offer refreshments and local delicacies to beachgoers. The laid-back atmosphere and stunning natural scenery make Hagonoy Island Beach a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a tranquil and rejuvenating beach getaway in Siargao Island.

Sohoton Cove

Sohoton Cove are greeted by a breathtaking landscape of towering limestone cliffs draped in lush greenery, surrounded by serene waters teeming with marine life. The cove's emerald-colored lagoon, often referred to as the "Blue Lagoon," offers a tranquil oasis for swimming, kayaking, and snorkeling amidst stunning natural scenery. One of the highlights of Sohoton Cove is the captivating Sohoton Cove National Park, a protected area home to a network of majestic caves, including the famous Hagukan Cave and Magkukuob Cave. Exploring these caves reveals mesmerizing stalactite and stalagmite formations, underground rivers, and hidden lagoons, creating an otherworldly experience for adventurers.

Lake Mainit

Lake Mainit holds the distinction of being the largest lake in Mindanao and the fourth largest in the Philippines by surface area. Lake Mainit holds profound cultural significance for the indigenous communities and local inhabitants dwelling in its vicinity. Embedded within their traditions, folklore, and livelihoods, the lake embodies a timeless connection to their ancestral heritage, weaving a tapestry of cultural richness that adds depth to its allure.It stands as a symbol of natural splendor and ecological resilience, cherished for its vastness, biodiversity, cultural resonance, and potential for sustainable progress. As a cherished landmark of Mindanao, it continues to inspire awe and reverence among all who are fortunate enough to behold its magnificence.

Laswitan Falls and Lagoon

At the heart of Laswitan lies its namesake attraction, the Laswitan Falls, a cascading waterfall that descends gracefully into a serene lagoon below. Unlike traditional waterfalls, Laswitan's allure lies in its distinctive formation, where the water flows over rock formations, creating mesmerizing curtains of cascading water. Visitors are enchanted by the sight and sound of water gracefully tumbling over the rocks, creating a tranquil ambiance that invites contemplation and appreciation of nature's artistry. Laswitan holds cultural significance for the local community, who have cherished and preserved the site for generations. Visitors have the opportunity to engage with the friendly locals, learn about their traditions and customs, and gain insight into the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Sagisi Island

One of the most notable features of Sagisi Island is its idyllic beaches, adorned with powdery white sands and fringed by swaying coconut palms. Visitors are drawn to the island's tranquil shores, where they can soak up the sun, take leisurely walks along the coastline, or simply unwind in the serenity of the surroundings. Beyond its natural attractions, Sagisi Island holds cultural significance for the local communities who call it home. Visitors have the opportunity to engage with the friendly locals, learn about their traditions and way of life, and savor the warmth of Filipino hospitality.

San pedro cantiasay footbridge

The San Pedro Cantiasay Footbridge, located in the municipality of General Luna on Siargao Island in the Philippines, is a stunning architectural marvel and a beloved tourist attraction renowned for its natural beauty and unique design. Stretching gracefully over the clear turquoise waters of Cantiasay Bay, the footbridge connects the mainland to the nearby Cantiasay Islet, providing locals and visitors alike with access to the picturesque fishing village nestled on the islet's shores.

Battle of Surigao Strait Museum

The Battle of Surigao Strait Museum, located in Surigao City, Philippines, commemorates one of the most significant naval battles of World War II that took place in Surigao Strait on October 24-25, 1944. The museum serves as a poignant reminder of the bravery and sacrifice of those who fought in this historic event. The museum's exhibits chronicle the events leading up to the Battle of Surigao Strait, the strategies employed by the Allied and Japanese forces, and the outcomes of the battle. Visitors can explore artifacts, photographs, documents, and interactive displays that provide insights into the naval warfare tactics, weaponry, and the human stories behind the battle. One of the highlights of the museum is its collection of artifacts recovered from the underwater battlefield, including wreckage from sunken ships, ammunition, and personal belongings of the sailors who perished during the battle. These artifacts offer a tangible connection to the past and serve as a testament to the courage and sacrifice of the servicemen involved.

Nana's Beach

Nana's Beach is a pristine coastal haven nestled along the shores of Siargao Island in the Philippines. Renowned for its serene ambiance and natural beauty, Nana's Beach offers visitors a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Nana's Beach is also a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts, offering opportunities for surfing, stand-up paddleboarding, and snorkeling. Surrounded by crystal-clear waters teeming with marine life, the beach provides an immersive experience for those eager to explore the underwater world.

Basul Island

Basul Island, also known as Basul Parola, is a picturesque island located off the coast of Barangay Basul in the municipality of General Luna on Siargao Island, Philippines. Renowned for its stunning natural beauty and secluded ambiance, Basul Island is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by adventurous travelers. Basul Island is also celebrated for its pristine beaches, characterized by powdery white sands and crystal-clear waters. The island's tranquil shores provide an ideal setting for sunbathing, swimming, snorkeling, and beachcombing. The underwater world surrounding Basul Island is teeming with vibrant marine life, making it a popular spot for underwater enthusiasts to explore. Nature lovers will appreciate Basul Island's unspoiled landscapes and rich biodiversity. The island is home to diverse flora and fauna, including coconut palms, mangrove forests, and various bird species. Visitors can embark on nature walks, birdwatching expeditions, and eco-tours to discover the island's natural treasures.

Bao-bao Falls

Bao-bao is surrounded by verdant forests teeming with diverse flora and fauna, adding to the beauty and tranquility of the natural surroundings. Visitors can also explore nearby hiking trails, picnic areas, and viewpoints offering panoramic vistas of the waterfall and its surrounding landscape. Visitors to Bao-bao can enjoy various activities and experiences, including swimming in the pool at the base of the falls, riding bamboo rafts to get closer to the cascades, and taking a refreshing shower under the waterfall's cascading waters. Adventurous travelers can also hike to the upper tiers of the falls, where smaller cascades and natural pools await exploration.

Silop Cave

Silop Cave is a unique geological formation characterized by its underground river system, stunning limestone formations, and crystal-clear pools. The cave is accessible via a short hike through the jungle, offering visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the island's lush tropical landscapes. Once inside the cave, visitors can marvel at the intricate stalactites and stalagmites that adorn the caverns, formed over thousands of years by the slow drip of mineral-rich water. The underground river that flows through the cave creates a serene atmosphere, with the sound of flowing water echoing off the cave walls. Silop Cave offers a memorable and immersive experience for travelers seeking to discover the hidden treasures of Siargao Island. It is a testament to the island's natural beauty and rich biodiversity, showcasing the wonders of its underground world.

Libuacan Cold Spring

Libuacan Cold Spring is a refreshing natural attraction located in the municipality of Pilar on Siargao Island, Philippines. Renowned for its crystal-clear waters and invigorating ambiance, Libuacan Cold Spring is a popular destination for both locals and tourists seeking respite from the tropical heat. The spring's pristine waters flow into a series of shallow pools, creating a picturesque setting surrounded by tropical foliage and limestone formations. Visitors can wade into the pools, swim, or simply relax and soak up the natural beauty of the surroundings. Libuacan Cold Spring is also a favorite spot for picnics and gatherings, with shaded areas and picnic tables available for visitors to enjoy a leisurely meal or snack amidst the tranquil ambiance of the spring.

Surigao City Boulevard

Surigao City Boulevard serves as a hub for recreational activities and social gatherings, with open spaces for picnics, outdoor events, and cultural performances. It is a favorite spot for families, friends, and couples to gather and enjoy quality time together amidst the scenic beauty of the coastline. At night, the boulevard comes alive with colorful lights and vibrant activity. Street vendors set up stalls selling local snacks, refreshments, and souvenirs, adding to the bustling atmosphere of the area. Visitors can also enjoy the mesmerizing sight of the sunset over the horizon, painting the sky with hues of orange and pink. Surigao City Boulevard is not only a place for relaxation and recreation but also a cultural and historical landmark. It features monuments, statues, and markers commemorating significant events and personalities in Surigao's history, including national hero Jose Rizal.

Looc Beach

Looc Beach is also a haven for water sports enthusiasts, offering opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, kayaking, and paddleboarding. The calm and shallow waters of the bay provide a safe and enjoyable environment for aquatic activities, making it suitable for visitors of all ages and skill levels. Nature lovers will appreciate the rich biodiversity surrounding Looc Beach, with lush mangrove forests and vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life. Exploring the underwater world reveals a kaleidoscope of colors, with colorful fish, coral formations, and other marine creatures thriving in the bay's pristine waters.
